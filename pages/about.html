About Me

A bit about who I am, how I got here, and what I'm working on.

Who I am

I'm Angel Guzman, a Computer Science graduate from San Diego State University (BS, May 2025). I'm based in San Diego, CA and focused on backend systems, distributed applications, and anything that touches the hardware layer.

I enjoy understanding how systems actually work — from TCP/IP and container networking to microcontroller memory constraints. I'm comfortable debugging production issues at 2am as much as designing clean REST APIs on a whiteboard.

What I build

Most of my work lives in the backend. I've built containerised Python services with Docker and deployed them in cloud and on-prem Linux environments. I've also done embedded work — writing C++ for ESP32 microcontrollers and wiring up IoT sensor systems with Raspberry Pi and Arduino.

Projects I'm particularly proud of: a distributed Flask + Redis lab environment that simulates real failure modes, a wireless classroom air quality monitor with SMS alerts, and a Digimon virtual pet running on an ESP32 with custom sprites — just for the joy of it.

Current focus

Building out the backend platform for CapyTechnologies — a social media automation tool with Celery async workers, Docker, and Google Cloud. I care about reliability, clean API design, and systems that don't fall over under load.

Also open to backend, systems, or IoT roles in San Diego or remote. If you're working on something interesting in distributed systems, embedded hardware, or developer tooling — reach out.
